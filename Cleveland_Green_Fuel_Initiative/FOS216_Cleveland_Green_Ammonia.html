Section 01 — Water-to-Ammonia Process Chain

From Lake Erie to Fertilizer

A fully integrated five-stage conversion chain transforms Lake Erie freshwater into green ammonia and finished fertilizer products — with carbon intensity 18× lower than conventional grey ammonia production.

01 / INTAKE
Lake Erie Water Intake
Submerged crib 300–600m offshore. Below thermocline. Fish exclusion screens ≤0.15 m/s approach velocity. 800K L/day raw draw.
H₂O raw
02 / TREATMENT
Water Treatment Train
Ion exchange softening → reverse osmosis → deionisation polishing to <1 µS/cm conductivity. Electrolysis-grade feed.
H₂O ultrapure
03 / ELECTROLYSIS
PEM Electrolyser Farm
100 MW proton exchange membrane. 50 kWh/kg system efficiency. 20 MT H₂/day. O₂ byproduct captured for Cleveland Clinic sales + discharge enrichment.
2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
04 / N₂ SOURCE
Air Separation Unit
Cryogenic ASU produces 99.99% pure N₂ at ~78 MT/day. Phase 1: pipeline supply (Air Products/Linde). Phase 2: on-site ASU.
Air → 99.99% N₂
05 / SYNTHESIS
Haber-Bosch Loop
H₂:N₂ = 3:1. Iron catalyst (promoted). 400–500°C, 150–300 bar. Single-pass 15–25% conversion. Recycle loop. 452 MT NH₃/day output.
N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃
06 / PRODUCT
Finishing & Storage
Liquid NH₃ condensed at −33°C (atmospheric) or pressurised vessels. Urea prilling, UAN blending, MAP/DAP granulation downstream.
NH₃ → Urea, UAN, MAP

FastOx CO₂ integration advantage: the Sierra Energy FastOx gasifier (Zone 3) converts Cleveland MSW to syngas, producing a CO₂-rich off-gas stream. Captured CO₂ feeds directly to the urea synthesis unit — NH₃ + CO₂ → CO(NH₂)₂ — eliminating external CO₂ purchase and producing granular urea at near-zero feedstock cost. No standalone green ammonia producer in North America can replicate this integration.

Section 04 — Ammonia as Zero-Carbon Fuel

Marine, Industrial & Grid Fuel Applications

Green ammonia is not only a fertilizer feedstock. It is the most practical zero-carbon energy carrier for applications that cannot be electrified: deep-sea shipping, industrial process heat, and dispatchable grid generation.

⚓

Great Lakes Marine Bunkering

Zone 8 marine pier — Great Lakes' first green hydrogen and liquid ammonia bunkering facility. Serves the IMO 2050 decarbonisation mandate for the Great Lakes bulk carrier, container, and passenger ferry fleet.

Location: NE corner of Burke — Army Corps bulkhead transfer ~2029
Products: Compressed H₂ + cryogenic LNH₃ (−33°C)
Target customers: US Steel lake freighters, Algoma Central vessels, Cleveland–Ontario ferry
Revenue: ~$22M/year at full build
First-mover: No Great Lakes port currently offers green marine bunkering
🏭

Industrial Corridor Pipeline Fuel

NH₃ pipeline from Burke into the Northeast Ohio industrial corridor. Ammonia is a direct substitute for natural gas in industrial process burners and turbines rated for hydrogen-ammonia blends.

Kawasaki L30A: 100% ammonia fuel capable — commercially available gas turbine
Industrial Valley campus: pipeline connection to KARNO/turbine installations
Opportunity Corridor IPGD: KARNO + Mainspring LGen ammonia-blend capable
Regulatory driver: Ohio EPA Subpart OOO — NH₃ replaces natural gas with no equipment change
Carbon credit: Ohio Scope 1 elimination for industrial buyers
⚡

Grid-Scale Dispatchable Generation

Ammonia cracked to H₂ at point of use, fed to fuel cells or H₂-capable turbines for dispatchable zero-carbon grid power. The 72-hour NH₃ buffer tank acts as a virtual battery for demand response and PJM capacity market participation.

Energy stored: 72-hr buffer ≈ 500 MWh equivalent
KARNO operation: 200 kW modules operable on H₂/NH₃ blend
Grid service: PJM capacity market + ancillary services + demand response
NH₃ cracking: Ruthenium catalyst, 400°C → H₂ for fuel cell or turbine
Revenue: Grid power sales ~$35M/year (KARNO + grid export)

Section 07 — Regulatory Pathway

Permit Inventory & Political Strategy

Eight primary permits across five agencies. The Great Lakes Compact withdrawal is the longest lead-time item — a pre-application conference with Ohio DNR is the first recommended action.

Ohio DNR · 2–3 years
Great Lakes Compact Water Withdrawal
Consumptive use determination required. Ecological benefit argument (O₂ discharge enrichment — net-positive lake water impact) is centerpiece of application. Approval requires all eight GL states plus two Canadian provinces.
⏱ START IMMEDIATELY — longest lead-time item
Ohio EPA · 1–2 years
NPDES Discharge Permit
Required for any return flow to Lake Erie. Key issue: thermal discharge limits and dissolved oxygen requirements. Dissolved oxygen enrichment via electrolysis O₂ byproduct injection targets 12–14 mg/L discharge DO — above baseline lake conditions.
⏱ File at Phase 0 FEED completion
Ohio EPA DAP · 18 months
Air Construction Permit (NH₃)
Fugitive NH₃ emissions from storage tanks, loading operations, and process venting. Title V major source determination likely at 165,000 MT/yr scale. BACT analysis required for synthesis plant.
⏱ File concurrent with Phase 1 design
DOT PHMSA · 12 months
NH₃ Storage Design — 49 CFR Part 193
Pressurised and refrigerated anhydrous ammonia storage tank design code compliance. Pressure relief, emergency shutdown, leak detection, and security system requirements for large-volume NH₃ storage at lakefront site.
⏱ Parallel with Phase 1 detailed design
FERC · 2–3 years
Grid Interconnection (Offshore Wind)
Icebreaker Wind submarine cable FERC interconnection and PPA filing. Phase 4 trigger. Queue position filing should begin no later than Phase 2 to preserve interconnection slot for Phase 4 timeline.
⏱ Queue filing: Phase 2 commencement
FAA / Congress · Legislative
Burke Airport Closure
Grant Assurance 25 requires Congressional action or FAA waiver. Political reframe is essential: the ask is not "close an airport for a park" — it is "close an airport to build a DOE Hydrogen Hub anchor site and CHIPS Act data center." This is an ask Congress can champion.
⏱ Congressional outreach begins Phase 0
Cuyahoga County / City · 12 months
Industrial Zoning Amendment
Burke Airport overlay district amendment to permit heavy industrial, chemical processing, and energy infrastructure. Coordinated with FAA closure timeline. City Council legislative action required.
⏱ File with Phase 0 FEED submission
US Army Corps · 18 months
Section 404 — Barge Terminal
Cuyahoga River dredging, bulkhead construction, and in-water work for Zone 8 marine bunkering pier. Army Corps confined disposal area transfer to Port authority (~2029) precedes permit filing. Section 408 modification of existing federal project may also apply.
⏱ File Phase 2 — construction Phase 3

The FixOurStreets216 Project's core political insight: every prior Burke closure attempt has asked Congress and the FAA to close an airport for recreational or residential development — an ask that offers Congress nothing. The correct ask is to close Burke to build a nationally significant clean energy infrastructure project. DOE Hydrogen Hub + CHIPS Act data center + IMO 2050 marine fuel hub = three federal programs with Congressional champions Cleveland can activate simultaneously.

Section 09 — Competitive Analysis
