How a route comes together

Four steps, start to close-out — the same loop a crew lead runs every working day of the season.

01 / Map

Pull the lots

The vacant-lot registry lands on a live parcel map, sorted by priority, status, and ward. Filter to today's work in a tap.

02 / Build

Order the stops

The optimizer sequences the day for the least windshield time, then balances the load so no crew runs long.

03 / Dispatch

Hand it to the crew

Assign by capacity and send turn-by-turn stops to the field — high-priority and overdue parcels stay up top.

04 / Log

Close it out

Crews shoot before & after photos on site. Status and proof of work sync straight back to Cityworks AMS.

What's under the hood

Built for the truck, not the office

Everything a crew needs on a cracked phone screen in a dusty cab — and everything a supervisor needs back at the yard.

Live parcel map

Every vacant lot plotted by GIS, color-coded by priority and work status.

Route optimization

Shortest-drive sequencing across all of a day's stops, recalculated on the fly.

Crew load balancing

Assign by capacity so the day spreads evenly and nobody runs over hours.

Before & after photos

Date-stamped proof of work on every parcel, paired and stored against the lot.

Cityworks AMS push

Status, photos, and completions flow back to the system of record — single or batch.

Works offline

Installs like an app and keeps running where the signal drops — syncs when it's back.
