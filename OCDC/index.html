The mandate

Four things every facility must answer for

Cleveland's framework exists because large data centers press on the public systems — and the neighborhoods — around them. The ODCD will not issue a permit until all four are resolved.

01 / WATER

Lake Erie comes first

Evaporative cooling that draws down Lake Erie is disfavored. Closed-loop designs that return water to source are the expectation, with thermal and oxygen limits enforced at the outfall.

02 / POWER

Carry your own load

Facilities cannot simply connect a hyperscale load to the public grid. On-site generation requirements scale with tier, rising to 75% at campus scale and dedicated generation at gigascale.

03 / DISCHARGE

Nothing illegal to the sewer

Process cooling water is categorically prohibited from the NEORSD collection system. Every discharge path is permitted, metered, and monitored — or the facility does not open.

04 / NOISE

Sound stays on site

Cooling plant, generators, and switchgear all carry sound. Facility noise must be contained within the property line — measured at the boundary, not the source. The neighborhood does not hear the data center.

The path

From inquiry to permit

One office, one file, one accountable authority from first contact through issuance.

STEP 01

Pre-Evaluation

A three-part screening establishes tier, runs the load and water matrix, and confirms feasibility before any plans are drawn. No surprises at review.

STEP 02

Plan Review

Six disciplines review in parallel — building, power, water, sewer, environmental, and sustainability. Deficiencies return on a defined response clock.

STEP 03

Permit Issuance

The ODCD issues the consolidated permit and holds monitoring authority for the life of the facility. Compliance is continuous, not one-time.
