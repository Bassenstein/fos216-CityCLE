Purpose & Authority

Why the ODCD Exists

The ODCD is the primary municipal authority for all data center development within Cleveland city limits. It operates as a coordinating department — receiving a single application from an operator and routing it concurrently to every agency required to approve the project, eliminating sequential filing and providing a unified, accountable point of contact from first inquiry through certificate of occupancy.
1
Water Stewardship Unconstrained data center development risks unsustainable Lake Erie freshwater consumption and unauthorized thermal discharge into the NEORSD collection system.
2
Grid Reliability Unplanned high-density load additions degrade power reliability for residential and commercial ratepayers served by Cleveland Public Power and FirstEnergy/CEI.
3
Sewer Protection Process cooling water discharge into the NEORSD system is prohibited. The ODCD enforces this at permit stage — before a shovel enters the ground.
Department Structure

How the ODCD Is Organized

The ODCD is established within the Department of Building & Housing as a dedicated division with its own permitting authority, inspection staff, and inter-agency coordination mandate. Unlike a general building permit office, the ODCD holds technical subject-matter expertise in power infrastructure, water systems, and environmental compliance — enabling it to evaluate complex data center proposals as a first-order reviewer, not a pass-through.

The ODCD Director reports directly to the Commissioner of Building & Housing and coordinates standing working relationships with the Division of Water, Cleveland Public Power, FirstEnergy/CEI, NEORSD, Ohio EPA, and the Mayor's Office of Sustainability. Every data center permit issued in Cleveland carries the ODCD seal.
📋

The ODCD is the sole issuing authority for data center building permits, certificates of occupancy, and annual operating licenses within Cleveland city limits.
🔄

A single application filed with the ODCD is simultaneously distributed to all six partner agencies. No operator files independently with any partner agency.

Ordinance Authority
The ODCD operates under Ordinance DCO-2025-001, adopted by Cleveland City Council. The ordinance grants the ODCD statutory authority to condition, deny, suspend, or revoke any data center building permit, certificate of occupancy, or operating license without further Council action. Civil penalties are collectible as municipal debt. Enforcement actions are posted publicly to the ODCD permit portal within 48 hours of issuance.

Step 1 of 3 — Before You Apply

Pre-Evaluation & Site Screening

The ODCD Pre-Evaluation process is the mandatory first engagement between a prospective data center operator and the City of Cleveland. It is not a courtesy — it is a required prerequisite before any permit application is accepted. Pre-Evaluation protects both the operator and the City: operators receive a binding Tier Determination and a list of agency conditions before investing in full design documents, and the City ensures that no application enters formal review without passing a baseline feasibility screen.

The Pre-Evaluation process has three sequential steps: the Site Screening Form, the ODCD Technical Intake Meeting, and issuance of the Preliminary Tier Determination Letter. Only after receiving a valid PTD Letter may an operator submit Form DCO-APP-001.

Three-Step Pre-Evaluation Sequence
01
Site Screening Form (SSF)
Operator submits the two-page ODCD Site Screening Form online. The SSF captures: proposed site address and parcel ID, estimated peak IT load (kW), proposed cooling architecture (air-side, water-side, immersion, or hybrid), proposed power supply (utility grid, on-site generation type, or combination), and contact information. No fee. ODCD reviews within 5 business days and issues either a Completeness Confirmation or a Request for Clarification.
Timeline: 5 business days · No fee · Online submission
02
ODCD Technical Intake Meeting
Required meeting held at 601 Lakeside Ave or via ODCD video portal. Attendees must include the operator's representative, mechanical/electrical engineer of record (or equivalent), and — for Tier 3 through Tier 5 — the operator's licensed P.E. ODCD is represented by the Director or Senior Plan Reviewer, Grid & Utility Liaison, and the NEORSD Coordination Specialist. Topics covered: site constraints, cooling system concept, power supply pathway, NEORSD sewer exclusion plan, water intake source, and timeline expectations. Meeting minutes are circulated within 3 business days and form part of the record.
Timeline: Scheduled within 15 days of SSF confirmation
03
Preliminary Tier Determination Letter
Issued by the ODCD Director within 10 business days of the Technical Intake Meeting. The PTD Letter binds the City to the stated tier classification for 18 months from issuance, provided the operator does not materially change the proposed IT load or cooling architecture. The letter also includes: the list of required attachments for the formal application, the names of the assigned agency contacts at each of the six partner agencies, scope parameters for the Water Impact Study and Grid Interconnection Study, and any site-specific conditions identified during the intake meeting.
Issued within 10 business days · Valid 18 months

Step 2 of 3 — Formal Application Review

ODCD Plan Review Process

Plan Review is the core technical function of the ODCD. After an operator submits Form DCO-APP-001 and all required attachments, the ODCD Senior Plan Reviewers conduct a structured multi-discipline review across six technical domains: structural/architectural, mechanical/cooling, electrical/power, plumbing/drainage, environmental/water, and life safety/fire protection.

The ODCD does not wait for partner agencies before beginning its own review. ODCD technical review runs concurrently with agency review. The ODCD Plan Review team is the integrating body — it identifies conflicts, gaps, and conditions across all agency comments and assembles a single Consolidated Review Response (CRR) for the operator. No partner agency may issue independent permit denials, approvals, or conditions directly to the operator during active ODCD review.

This model gives operators a single voice from the City, eliminates conflicting agency directives, and establishes clear accountability: if the project stalls in review, the operator has one number to call.

Deficiency Response Process
1
Deficiency Letter Issued ODCD issues a single consolidated Deficiency Letter listing all items from all six agencies. Review clock is paused at the date of issuance.
2
Operator Response Window Operator has 21 calendar days to submit a Response to Deficiency package. One 14-day extension may be requested in writing before the deadline.
3
ODCD Adequacy Review ODCD reviews the response and determines adequacy within 10 business days. If adequate, review clock restarts with remaining days. If inadequate, a second — and final — deficiency letter is issued.
4
Second Deficiency / Denial Failure to adequately resolve all items on the second deficiency letter results in application denial. A denied application may be resubmitted as a new application with new fees after 90 days.
What Triggers Automatic Rejection
✕
No Valid PTD Letter Application submitted without a current Preliminary Tier Determination Letter is returned unreviewed within 5 business days. Pre-Evaluation step is mandatory.
✕
Process Drain to NEORSD Shown on Plans Any plan set showing a process cooling water drain connection to the NEORSD collection system triggers immediate rejection and a Sewer Risk Notice to NEORSD.
✕
IT Load Inconsistency If declared IT load in Section 2 is inconsistent with rack count, power density, and UPS sizing shown on the electrical plans by more than 15%, ODCD issues a Tier Reclassification Notice and rejects the application pending re-submission at the correct tier.
✕
Missing P.E. Seal (Tier 3, 4, 5) Water Impact Study, cooling system design, and power supply documentation for Tier 3 and above must carry an Ohio-registered P.E. seal. Unsealed documents are rejected at completeness check.

Coordinating Partners

Six-Agency Partnership

The ODCD leads — every partner agency receives the application from ODCD, routes comments back to ODCD, and issues no independent directives to the operator. One interface. One voice.

ODCD
Office of Data Center Development (Lead)
Primary permit authority, plan review lead, inspection authority, enforcement, CO and annual license issuance. All other agencies report review findings to ODCD — not directly to the operator.
DOW
Cleveland Division of Water
Water intake metering, discharge oversight, Lake Erie thermal compliance monitoring, and GLWA coordination for high-volume withdrawals. Review findings submitted to ODCD within the review clock.
CPP
Cleveland Public Power / FirstEnergy CEI
Grid interconnection coordination, load curtailment agreement execution, PJM demand response enrollment verification, and on-site generation certification. Findings consolidated into ODCD CRR.
NEORSD
NE Ohio Regional Sewer District
Industrial pretreatment permitting, sewer exclusion enforcement, biocide and temperature monitoring, and performance bond oversight. Any NEORSD Sewer Risk Flag is communicated to operator through ODCD only.
EPA
Ohio Environmental Protection Agency
NPDES permit for any surface water discharge, thermal discharge compliance review, and air quality review for on-site generation systems above 1 MW. NPDES determination routed through ODCD CRR.
MOS
Mayor's Office of Sustainability
Renewable energy compliance verification, PPA and REC documentation review, and carbon-free power pathway approval for Tier 3–5 facilities. Sustainability certification incorporated in ODCD Conditional Building Permit.
ODCD Leads. Always.
No partner agency may issue a permit approval, conditional approval, denial, or enforcement notice directly to a data center operator without routing through the ODCD. This single-lead model eliminates the conflicting directives, duplicated fees, and scheduling conflicts that operators face when navigating multiple city agencies independently. The ODCD Director is accountable for the consolidated position of all six agencies on every application.
