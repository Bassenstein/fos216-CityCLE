THE PREMISE

The city spends real money on two things every year: drawing and treating Lake Erie water, and paying contractors to landfill its waste. Each is treated as a cost. Each can instead be run as a feedstock — and both roads end at the same place: green hydrogen.

The Green Fuel Initiative is not a single plant. It is a framing: two independent, federally-fundable projects that share inputs Cleveland already controls, share a common output molecule, and share the same logistics and incentive structure. Each has its own full feasibility study below.

Why It's One Initiative

Two Roads to the Same Molecule

Electrolysis and gasification look nothing alike on the front end. They land on the same product: hydrogen — and hydrogen is what makes both projects fundable under the same federal programs.

From Water · Electrolysis

Split the molecule with clean power

Renewable electricity drives a PEM electrolyser that splits ultra-pure Lake Erie water into hydrogen and oxygen. No carbon in the reaction.

  • Input: freshwater + renewable power
  • Reaction: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
  • Byproduct: oxygen (saleable)
H2
The shared output
Both pathways converge here — then route to ammonia, marine fuel, and grid-balancing offtake.
From Waste · Gasification

Pull the molecule out of trash

The FastOx gasifier heats municipal solid waste in a low-oxygen environment to produce hydrogen-rich syngas — diverting waste from the landfill instead of paying to bury it.

  • Input: municipal solid waste
  • Output: H₂-rich syngas + slag
  • Yield: ~78 kg H₂ per ton MSW
Offtake A — Fertilizer

Hydrogen → ammonia → green nitrogen fertilizer barged to the Corn Belt at a Great Lakes cost advantage.

Offtake B — Marine Fuel

Green ammonia as a zero-carbon bunker fuel for Great Lakes shipping ahead of IMO 2050 targets.

Offtake C — Direct H₂

Hydrogen for transit fleets, industrial users, and grid-balancing — sold under the same 45V credit structure.

Federal & Structural Alignment

The Two Projects Share an Incentive Stack

Because both pathways produce clean hydrogen, the same federal programs, logistics, and ownership model carry across — so one initiative can pursue them in parallel rather than competing for attention.

U.S. Dept. of Energy

Hydrogen Hub

Both pathways strengthen a Cleveland bid for DOE Hydrogen Hub designation and feasibility funding — diverse feedstocks make a stronger hub case.

Both pathways
Inflation Reduction Act

45V Clean H₂ Credit

The production tax credit for clean hydrogen — up to $3/kg — applies to electrolytic and waste-derived hydrogen alike, for up to ten years.
